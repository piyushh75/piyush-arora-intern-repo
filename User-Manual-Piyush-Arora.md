### 1. What is a "User Manual for Me," and why do teams use them?

**Definition:** A "User Manual for Me" is a personal document that outlines how you work best, what you value in professional relationships, and how others can best interact with you.

**Purpose:** Teams use them to bridge the gap between different personalities and work styles, especially in remote environments where traditional social cues are missing.

**Efficiency:** It accelerates the "getting to know you" phase of a new internship, allowing you to hit the ground running with your supervisor and peers.

### 2. What categories are helpful to include?

**Communication Style:** Do you prefer direct messages (Discord) or structured updates (Email)? Are you someone who likes small talk or someone who gets straight to business?

**Work Habits & Hours:** When are you most productive? Do you prefer deep-work blocks or frequent collaborative check-ins?

**Feedback Preferences:** Do you prefer constructive feedback in real-time during a meeting, or would you rather read it in a document first so you can process it?

**Quirks & Values:** What are your "pet peeves" (e.g., vague meeting invites) and what values drive your work (e.g., accountability)?

### 3. How can writing a personal user manual improve teamwork and reduce misunderstandings?

**Sets Clear Expectations:** It removes the guesswork. If your manual says you don't check messages after 6 PM, teammates won't feel ignored when you don't reply until morning.

**Builds Psychological Safety:** By being vulnerable about your preferences and weaknesses, you encourage others to be open as well, fostering a more inclusive environment.

**Prevents Conflict:** Many workplace conflicts arise from differing communication styles; a manual addresses these differences before they lead to friction.
