### Key Roles in an Agile Team

**Developers:** Responsible for the actual creation of the product. In an Agile environment, they are often self-organizing and focus on delivering functional increments of work.

**QA (Quality Assurance):** Ensures the software meets specific standards and is free of critical bugs. In high-security environments like Focus Bear, they often collaborate early with developers to provide immediate feedback on privacy and encryption.

**Product Manager (PM):** Defines the vision and priorities. They represent the voice of the customer and ensure the team is working on the most valuable tasks.

**Agile Project Manager (Agile PM):** Acts as a facilitator. They remove blockers, manage the workflow, and ensure the team adheres to Agile values like responding to change over following a rigid plan.

### Supporting Roles:

**Stakeholders:** Individuals outside the immediate team (like executives or investors) who have an interest in the project's outcome.

**UX (User Experience):** Focuses on the design and usability of the interface.

**Customer Support:** Provides real-time feedback from users, which in a Kanban system can lead to immediate task adjustments.

## Role Collaboration: Scrum vs. Kanban

### Scrum: Structured and Role-Specific

**Defined Roles:** Scrum requires specific roles, most notably the Scrum Master (who facilitates the process) and the Product Owner (who manages the backlog).

**Fixed Cycles:** The team collaborates within fixed-length "Sprints" (usually 14 days).

**Commitment:** At the start of a sprint, roles collaborate to commit to a specific set of tasks (the Sprint Backlog), and the scope is generally locked until the cycle ends.

### Kanban: Fluid and Continuous

**Flexible Roles:** Kanban is less rigid about specific titles. While people still have specialized skills (like Security or Development), the focus is on the continuous flow of tasks.

**WIP Limits:** Everyone collaborates to ensure that the "Work-In-Progress" (WIP) limits are respected. If a bottleneck occurs in QA, developers might stop taking new tasks to help clear the backlog.

**Immediate Response:** Roles interact based on real-time needs. For example, a security analyst can audit a small code change as soon as a developer moves it to the next column, rather than waiting for a sprint review.

### 1. What is my role, and how does it contribute to the Agile process?

As a cybersecurity professional at Focus Bear, my role is to ensure that "working software" is also "secure software". I contribute to the Agile process by implementing a "Shift Left" strategy, which means I audit small code changes as they happen rather than waiting for a massive, finished product at the end. This approach allows for technical adaptability, where I can shift focus instantly based on real-time security data or user needs to keep the app resilient.

### 2. How do my responsibilities overlap or interact with other roles?

My work requires constant synchronization with the team to ensure we are aligned and moving in the right direction.

**Developers:** I provide immediate feedback on encryption and data privacy, which helps developers catch small mistakes before they become expensive security flaws.

**QA (Quality Assurance):** We overlap in the validation phase; while QA focuses on functionality, I ensure the increments meet security compliance and maintain a clear audit trail.

**Product Manager:** I interact with the PM to ensure security tasks are integrated into the workflow, helping to manage project scope and prevent it from growing unintentionally.

**Agile PM:** We collaborate on operational transparency. By visualizing progress and security blockers daily on the Kanban board, we reduce confusion and keep the entire team aligned.
