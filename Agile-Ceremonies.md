## The Main Agile Ceremonies & Their Purpose

Agile ceremonies are "checkpoints" that ensure the team stays aligned and keeps improving.

**Daily Stand-ups:** A quick, 15-minute meeting where everyone shares what they did yesterday, what they'll do today, and any "blockers" (problems) in their way. It's for synchronization, not status reporting.

**Sprint Planning vs. Continuous Prioritization:**

- **Sprint Planning (Scrum):** The team sits down before a 2-week block to decide exactly which tasks they can commit to finishing.
- **Continuous Prioritization (Kanban):** Since Kanban is a flow, the team looks at the "Backlog" constantly and moves the most important task to the top so it's ready to be "pulled" next.

**Retrospectives:** A meeting at the end of a cycle to look back on how the team worked. We ask: What went well? What didn't? How can we improve our process next time?

**Backlog Refinement:** Cleaning up the "To-Do" list. We break down big tasks into smaller ones and make sure every ticket has enough detail for a developer to start working.

## Asynchronous & Cross-Time Zone Collaboration

Since Agile teams (like Focus Bear) often work across different time zones, we can't always meet in person. We collaborate through:

**The Kanban Board:** The board is the "Source of Truth." If I update a task in my time zone, a teammate in a different time zone sees the progress immediately without needing an email.

**Written Updates:** Using GitHub comments or Slack/Discord for "Async Stand-ups." Instead of a meeting, everyone posts their 3-point update at the start of their day.

**Recorded Demos:** Instead of a live meeting to show a new feature, we record a quick video (like a Loom) so the team can watch it when they wake up.

**Clear Documentation:** Writing down decisions in Markdown files (like this one!) ensures that everyone has access to the same information, regardless of when they are online.

## How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?

Agile ceremonies act as the "heartbeat" of a project, ensuring that communication is consistent rather than accidental. They help with alignment by:

**Breaking Down Silos:** Meetings like the Daily Stand-up ensure everyone knows what others are working on, preventing two people from doing the same work.

**Creating a Feedback Loop:** Retrospectives allow the team to discuss what is working and what isn't, ensuring the process evolves to meet the team's needs.

**Maintaining a Shared Goal:** Sprint Planning and Backlog Refinement keep everyone focused on the same priorities, so the team isn't just "busy," but is moving toward the most important objectives.
